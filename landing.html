Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Heavy Metal Pollution Index (HPI)?

HPI is a numerical value that indicates the overall level of heavy metal contamination in water, calculated using concentrations of various metals and their permissible limits.

Why is monitoring heavy metals important?

Heavy metals are toxic pollutants that can accumulate in living organisms, causing serious health and environmental problems. Monitoring helps in early detection and mitigation.

How accurate is this calculator?

The calculator uses standardized formulas and permissible limits from recognized authorities, providing reliable estimates based on input data.

Can I use this tool for any water source?

Yes, as long as you have the concentration data of the heavy metals, you can use this tool to assess pollution levels in groundwater, surface water, or other sources.

What should I do if the HPI is high?

High HPI indicates pollution above safe limits. You should report to local environmental authorities and avoid using contaminated water until treated.

Where can I report heavy metal pollution complaints?

You can contact government environmental agencies via the contact section below for complaints and assistance.
